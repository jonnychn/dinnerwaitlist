What makes this dinner different

Depth over noise

Most networking dinners mean loud music, rushed small talk, and a handful of business cards you never use. You leave without any real insight or connection.

Typical networking

More noise, less meaning

Crowded rooms, shallow chat, people selling or pitching, and no real outcomes beyond a tired smile and a bigger stack of cards.

Purpose Club

A quiet room for honest leaders

Curated guests, guided questions, and themes that invite reflection. No solicitation. No random selling. Just space to think, listen, and speak openly.

Who is this for

The right room

Executives, professionals, and educators from technology, finance, consulting, education, and startups. Many describe themselves as quiet high performers or introverts who usually do not open up at events.

  • You want fewer but deeper relationships, not more shallow contacts.
  • You are willing to listen as much as you speak.
  • You value purpose, self mastery, and reflection, not just performance.
  • You are open to sharing real stories, within clear boundaries and respect.

If that sounds like you, you will likely feel at home at this table.

What happens at dinner

Designed for introverts
Phone free, solicitation free, judgment free
  • Warm arrivals instead of awkward mingling. You are welcomed by name and never left standing alone.
  • Guided conversation instead of random chat. A host leads prompts that invite participation without pressure.
  • Vulnerable stories from the room about bankruptcy, divorce, depression, reinvention, and starting again.
  • Clear takeaways. You leave with new questions, practical insight, and relationships that continue after the evening.

Themes we explore

Beyond job titles

Each dinner centers around one core question, such as:

  • Reinventing your life or city when the script no longer fits.
  • How to learn AI and stay relevant without burning out.
  • Leading through uncertainty when you are not sure you have the answers.
  • Turning setbacks into a new chapter instead of a permanent label.

The goal is not to impress the room. It is to leave a little more honest, a little less alone, and a little clearer about your next step.

Why we use a waitlist

Protecting the room

Purpose Club is intentionally small. Seats are limited so that:

  • Everyone has space to speak or quietly reflect.
  • The mix of backgrounds serves the whole table instead of one agenda.
  • The room stays safe, respectful, and non transactional for introverts and extroverts alike.

For that reason, not every request will be offered a seat at the next dinner. Your responses on the form help us understand if this is the right room for you and which theme or city might fit best.

If the timing is not right, we will keep you in mind for a future table rather than squeezing you into a room that is already full.
